The Behavioral Derailers of CEO Succession

Published: June 2026 • Author: Aurelius Governance Board

Why do over 40% of newly appointed Chief Executives fail within their first 18 months? Our research indicates the primary factor is rarely commercial competence; it is relational alignment and behavioral derailers.

When under intense pressure, even the most capable executives revert to baseline behaviors. If these baseline patterns contain unchecked 'derailers'—such as extreme micromanagement, aversion to feedback, or defensive isolation—they erode trust within the boardroom and executive committees.

"Relational derailers are not visible during ordinary operations. They manifest exclusively under systemic pressure, making advanced psychometric mapping critical."

Mapping the Critical Derailer Profiles

Through our work with Fortune 500 boards, we have mapped three primary behaviors that derail incoming officers:

  1. Defensive Isolation: A tendency to withdraw and filter incoming critical operational updates during market volatility, leading to siloed decision processes.
  2. Executive Micro-Management: Transitioning from high-level vision to detailed tactical control, diminishing executive trust levels.
  3. Boardroom Defiance: Disregarding alignment procedures in favor of unilateral actions, resulting in boardroom governance friction.

Strategic Recommendation for Governance Panels

Nominations committees must shift from simple interview checklists to empirical, multi-dimensional profiles. Integrating standardized Hogan assessment indicators and qualitative stakeholder reviews ensures incoming successors possess the relational maturity needed to match their commercial capabilities.
