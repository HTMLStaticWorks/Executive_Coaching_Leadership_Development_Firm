CEO Succession Continuity at Meridian Logistics

Restructuring leadership capability to protect market share and secure executive alignment during a multi-phase founder transition.

The Challenge

Meridian Logistics, a leading logistics enterprise, faced a transition crisis. The founder and CEO of 20 years announced plans to step down within 12 months. Early succession indicators showed high risk: the board was split on candidate profiles, and three key divisional presidents indicated they would leave the organization if they were bypassed for the role.

The Solution

Aurelius was engaged by the Nominations Committee. We designed a multi-stage alignment program:

  • Leadership Assessment: We deployed Hogan profiles and clinical 360-degree assessments for all internal prospects.
  • Board Alignment Summit: We ran facilitated board offsites to agree upon objective selection criteria.
  • Transition Mentoring: A comprehensive 10-month onboarding and behavioral transition program for the selected successor, alongside retention counseling for the divisional presidents.

The Outcomes

The transition succeeded without loss of operational speed or capital flight:

  • 100% Executive Retention: All division presidents were retained in aligned, expanded roles.
  • 96% Successor Rating: Successor alignment surveys at month 6 post-hand-off showed high score structures.
  • Investor Confidence: Stock prices remained stable throughout the transition sequence.
